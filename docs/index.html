Your sidebar is a graveyard of "New chat"

ChatGPT names conversations from your first message, so the sidebar fills with vague, duplicated, half-sentence titles. Months later, finding the right thread means scrolling forever.

Review, then rename

Nothing is changed without your confirmation.

1

Open the panel

Click the Threadsmith launcher on chatgpt.com. Scroll the sidebar to load more history, then hit Refresh to pull it into the list.

2

Generate previews

Pick conversations and generate clear title suggestions. Edit anything, or uncheck what you don't want.

3

Apply & undo

Apply the renames through ChatGPT's own editor. Changed your mind? Undo any title back to the original in one click.

Private by design

There is no Threadsmith server. The developer never sees your data.

  • Conversation text goes only to the AI provider you choose, with your own key.
  • Your settings and API key are stored locally in your browser.
  • No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry — ever.
  • Threadsmith only acts on the conversations you select, and only after you confirm.

Questions

Does it cost money?
Threadsmith is free and open source. You pay only your AI provider for the title-generation requests, which are tiny — with a provider like DeepSeek, renaming hundreds of chats costs a few cents.
What languages can it write titles in?
Simplified Chinese or English. Set it explicitly, or use Auto to match each conversation's language. Proper nouns, brands, and technical names are kept as-is.
Is it safe? Can I undo a rename?
Yes. You preview and confirm every title before it's applied, and each applied rename can be undone back to its original from the list. Threadsmith uses ChatGPT's own rename feature — it never touches conversation content.
Do you store or see my conversations?
No. There is no backend. Your conversation excerpts are sent directly from your browser to the AI provider you configured. Nothing is sent to the developer.
Do I need an API key?
Yes, for now. You add a key from a provider like DeepSeek or OpenAI once in settings. It's stored locally and used only to generate titles.
